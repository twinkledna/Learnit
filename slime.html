How to Make Slime

By Stephanie

Who doesn't love slime? It's squishy, stretchy, and oh-so-slimy! It's also super cheap, easy to make and, incredibly versatile and customizable. In this article, we'll teach you a simple way to make slime at home. All you need is a sticky slime base (usually glue) and a slime activator (typically borax or contact solution). Keep reading to learn how you can make your own slime with ingredients you likely already have lying around!

Materials

• Bowl

• Mixing tool

• Air-tight container

• Glue

• Contact lens solution

• (Optional) Shaving cream

• (Optional) Glitter/Food Colouring

Step 1:

Pour glue into a bowl. (Can be white, clear, or coloured.)

Step 2 (optional):

Optional: Add colour or glitter to the glue. (Use food colouring, paint, pigment powders, etc.) Then mix the glue until the colour/glitter is evenly distributed.

Step 3 (optional):

Optional: Add shaving cream to the glue mixture to make the slime more fluffy. Mix the shaving cream in until the mixture is even.

Step 4:

Add contact lens solution to the glue mixture. Make sure to add a few drops at a time, otherwise the slime will turn out too hard, and it will be difficult to play with.

Step 5:

Mix the glue mixture until the glue starts to clump together, and it pulls away from the bowl. Then knead the slime with your hands until it's not as sticky anymore. Add more contact lens solution or baking soda as needed.

Step 6:

Store in an air-tight container to keep it safe.

Expert Q&A

Question

What's the best way to store slime?

Community Answer

Slimes tend to expand after play, so you can store any leftover portions in an airtight container, like Tupperware or a Ziploc bag. That prevents air exposure, which dries it out. Once the slime settles back to its original size after a few days, you can return the leftovers to the original jar it came in. Always keep it sealed to avoid drying out. If it does harden, a bit of vinegar can help revive it.
